Don’t Trust Your Agents, Verify Them:
Strategic Verification with VITAMIN

Strategic reasoning and verification are key to building dependable multi-agent systems — especially in safety-critical, normative, and cooperative domains. This tutorial introduces VITAMIN, a modular and extensible framework for the strategic verification of MAS, from modelling choices to practical model checking workflows.

Get slides & materials Download the proposal (PDF)

What you’ll learn

  • How strategic properties differ from purely temporal properties (abilities, coalitions, adversaries).
  • How VITAMIN separates concerns between models, logics, and verification engines.
  • How to model a MAS in VITAMIN, write strategic specifications, and run verification tasks.
  • How to reason about verification trade-offs (expressiveness vs scalability) in practice.

Abstract

Many properties of interest in multi-agent systems concern not only how systems evolve over time, but also what agents (or coalitions) can achieve by choosing their actions strategically — e.g., coordination guarantees, enforceability of norms, robustness against misbehaviour, or the ability of a coalition to achieve goals despite adversarial behaviour.

This tutorial introduces VITAMIN (VerIficaTion of A MulTI-Agent system), a compositional and extensible framework designed to make strategic verification more accessible and practically usable. VITAMIN supports expressive temporal and strategic logics, heterogeneous agent models, and flexible verification workflows, with guided steps that help users construct models, specify properties, and execute verification tasks.

Detailed outline

Proposed as a half-day in-person tutorial with approximately 3.5 hours of technical content.

30 min
Introduction and Motivation
Why strategic verification for MAS? From temporal properties to strategic abilities. Application domains and challenges.
45 min
Background: Strategic Logics and Agent Models
Temporal and strategic logics (e.g., CTL, ATL). Concurrent game structures. Strategies, coalitions, objectives. Modelling assumptions and limitations.
45 min
Strategic Verification in VITAMIN
Architecture and components. Supported logics and tasks. Verification workflows.
60 min
Hands-on Modelling and Verification
Modelling a MAS in VITAMIN. Writing strategic specifications. Executing verification tasks. Analysing results.
30 min
Discussion and Outlook
Open research challenges. Industrial relevance. Q&A.

Target audience

Researchers, PhD students, and practitioners working on multi-agent systems, formal methods, and autonomous systems. Advanced MSc students with a background in AI or distributed systems may also benefit.

Prerequisites

  • Basic knowledge of multi-agent systems.
  • Familiarity with temporal logic is helpful but not required.
  • No prior experience with strategic logics or VITAMIN is expected.
